The Fresh Basket Journey

Fresh Basket began in 2010 with a simple mission: to connect people with fresh, organic, and sustainably-sourced food. Founded by Elizabeth and Michael Chen, our journey started at a small farmers' market in Portland, where we built relationships with local farmers who shared our passion for quality and sustainability.

What began as a weekend market stall has grown into a thriving business with multiple locations across the Pacific Northwest. Throughout our growth, we've remained true to our core values: supporting local farmers, promoting sustainable agriculture, and making healthy food accessible to all.

Today, Fresh Basket continues to innovate while honoring our roots. We've expanded our product range to include thousands of carefully selected items, but our commitment to quality, sustainability, and community remains unchanged.

Our Growth Timeline

2010

Fresh Basket founded by Elizabeth and Michael Chen as a weekend farmers' market stall in Portland.

2012

Opened our first brick-and-mortar store in Portland's Pearl District, focusing on local and organic produce.

2015

Expanded product range to include artisanal bakery, dairy, and sustainable meat options. Launched our community garden initiative.

2018

Opened three additional locations across Oregon and Washington. Started our farm-to-school program supporting local education.

2020

Launched our e-commerce platform and home delivery service, making fresh food accessible during challenging times.

2025

Today, Fresh Basket operates 12 stores across the Pacific Northwest with a thriving online presence, serving over 100,000 customers monthly.

Our Mission & Values

At Fresh Basket, we're guided by a clear mission and strong values that shape everything we do.

Sustainability

We're committed to environmentally responsible practices throughout our supply chain, from farm to table. We minimize waste, reduce packaging, and prioritize renewable resources.

Community

We believe in supporting local economies and building strong communities. We partner with local farmers, artisans, and organizations to create a positive impact where we live and work.

Quality

We never compromise on quality. Every product in our stores meets our strict standards for freshness, taste, and nutritional value. We believe good food is the foundation of a good life.

Our Mission

"To make fresh, organic, and sustainably-sourced food accessible to all, while supporting local farmers, promoting environmental stewardship, and building stronger communities."

Our Commitment to Sustainability

Sustainability isn't just a buzzword at Fresh Basket—it's at the core of everything we do.

Organic Farming Practices

We partner exclusively with farmers who follow organic and regenerative agricultural practices. This means no synthetic pesticides or fertilizers, crop rotation to maintain soil health, and farming methods that protect biodiversity.

Our farm partners work with nature, not against it, resulting in healthier ecosystems and more nutritious food. We regularly visit our partner farms to ensure they maintain our high standards for sustainable farming.

Waste Reduction Efforts

We're committed to minimizing waste throughout our operations. Our stores feature bulk food sections that reduce packaging waste, and we encourage customers to bring reusable bags and containers.

Unsold but still edible food is donated to local food banks and community organizations. Food scraps are composted, and we've implemented comprehensive recycling programs at all locations. Our goal is to achieve zero waste across all operations by 2030.

Local Sourcing Commitment

We prioritize locally-grown and produced items whenever possible. At least 60% of our products come from within a 250-mile radius of each store, reducing transportation emissions and supporting local economies.

Our local sourcing program has helped launch dozens of small food businesses and kept family farms thriving. We're proud to serve as a bridge between local producers and conscious consumers who value fresh, sustainable food.
